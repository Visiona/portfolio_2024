CONTEXT

In 2016, I was approached by a friend to assist with the website for a unique Fellowship he was part of: the Polish-speaking Alcoholics Anonymous in Ireland. This organization is a branch of a global group dedicated to supporting individuals facing the challenges of alcoholism through the shared strength, hope, and experience of the 12-step program. Their website serves as an extension of their mission, providing resources and support for their members.



GOALS

  • Create a redesigned site that aligns with the latest trends and is optimised for mobile and tablet devices.
  • Restructure the entire information architecture to ensure the content hierarchy meets user needs.
  • Collaborate on creating essential content copy.
  • Make the website user-centered and accessible, supporting AA’s 7th tradition of “Carrying the message to the alcoholic who still suffers.”
  • Position site so it's easy to find via modern search engines and social media


PROJECT CONSTRAINTS

The website for the Polish-speaking Alcoholics Anonymous in Ireland faces several unique challenges and constraints:

  • Rotating Management: The website is managed by different AA members every two years, leading to varying levels of technical familiarity among its overseers.
  • User Demographics: The users include individuals with limited internet access and beginners in online navigation.
  • Adherence to AA's 12 Traditions: The website design must align with AA’s principles, such as attraction over promotion, neutrality on external issues, and maintaining anonymity.
  • Design Guidelines: There are no official AA design style guides, but design choices are limited to the colors and fonts commonly used in AA publications, respecting each group's autonomy.
  • Platform Limitations: Hosted on Jimdo for cost-effectiveness and user-friendliness, the website faces restrictions in modifying its structure and appearance due to the platform’s constraints.

HIGH FIDELITY PROTOTYPE & INTERACTION

Here are some features incorporated into the prototype based on the wireframe-testing results.

Usability heuristics at every step: Error Prevention while form is not completed, it is saved, can be edited now or later; User Control though control over unwanted actions, Minimalism of read content, Consistency

High color contrast ratio of 19:6 and AAA compliance ensure excellent readability and usability for all users, including those with visual impairments. This makes it easy to navigate and quickly access information, especially for users in a rush.

Enhanced accessibility and usability by showing the text field's border highlighted on focus, during typing, and when inactive, making it clear and intuitive for users to interact with the input area.

Visibility of System Status Heuristic - tracking remaining characters in all text fields for candidates, gives more control over content size to Support Team and helps candidates draft their answers better.

RESULTS

How the project affected users and the business

The interactive design of the form interface significantly reduced the need for detailed instructions, leading to a 75% decrease in the Leadership Support Team's workload. User feedback was overwhelmingly positive, with many appreciating the form's ease of use and reliability. For example, users noted that whatever they left unfinished in the form, their data remained intact even when their connection dropped during travel. This form has now become a benchmark for similar projects at Accenture.
