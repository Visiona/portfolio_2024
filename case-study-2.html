Onboarding Hub

February 2023
Scroll

My Role: User Experience Design, Interaction design
Team size: 4 (Project Manager, Product Owner, Web Developer )
Timeframe: Nov 2022 - Feb 2023



CONTEXT

The client had several sites intended for new joiners, but apparently they were at a loss in finding information scattered across these various platforms. During onboarding, new hires received emails with multiple links to access different resources. This fragmented approach led to poor engagement, a high volume of inquiries to the Careers team, and feedback from new hires indicating difficulty in finding the necessary information.



GOALS

  • Centralize Information & Improve Engagement: Consolidate scattered resources into a single, user-friendly platform to attract and retain new joiners.
  • Improve Information Accessibility: Design a cohesive information architecture that makes it easy for users to find the information they need.
  • Reduce Inquiries to Careers Team: Implement clear, easy-to-find FAQs and self-service resources to decrease the number of repetitive inquiries.
  • Enhance Onboarding Experience: Gather feedback and use it to continuously improve the platform’s content and navigation, ensuring new hires can easily locate essential information.


RESEARCH

To address the low engagement and high inquiry rate from the new joiners, I conducted a comprehensive UX research project utilising user interviews, usability testing, surveys, and analytics review.


User Interviews

Conducted two one-on-one interviews with recent new hires and members of the Careers team. Here is a snippet of some questions & answers:

  • Can you describe your experience accessing the onboarding resources?
  • What specific information did you find difficult to locate on the site?
  • How did you feel about the received induction materials during onboarding?

Survey

We found out that during onboarding workshops new joiners already filled out surveys. Here are some questions and more reoccuring findings.

  • On a scale of 1-10, how easy was it to find the information you needed?
  • What information was difficult to find?
  • How satisfied are you with the current onboarding portal?

Key Findings

  • As users become more familiar with the site, they should find relatively easier advanced materials, which are grouped deeper within the navigation.
  • Prioritise basic information in architecture of information
  • Use onboarding modules, quizzes, and virtual events for better engagement

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

After identifying potential solutions and reviewing all available related materials that HR can provide, I found it important to organise them and structure them using Information Architecture principles.

During the process of structuring new site map I realised the volume of information will need to be split into multiple sites; it would be too much for Home Page only.

I used Home Page as a high level view of all information and so from there users could navigate to detailed subsites.

LOW FIDELITY PROTOYPE & USABILITY TESTING

Using the results gathered during the interviews, sitemap & available materials I created new low fidelity prototype. I then invited a new cohort of 4 employees, again at varying levels and locations, to navigate these prototypes, hear their thoughts.

The users found it easy to accomplish what the interviewer was asking while they also raised the following issues and suggestions:

  • Beneficial would be to have few crucial links they use easy to refer to from teh site ie. Time & Expense site etc.
  • It would be easier to access materials related to particular events within the Agenda on the same site
  • They pointed to add additional topics within the resources, which they usually looked for

RESULTS

How the project affected users and the business

The impact on the onboarding process was significant, improving overall satisfaction among new joiners and even people involved in onboarding events. After the February 2023 onboarding round, the client confirmed that the portal greatly facilitated onboarding, saving approximately average of two hours per new hire and simplifying navigation within the intricacies of the new company. The original site was built originally intended only for UK, but it’s success affected Irish Team to replicate this site and structured adequately all their materials.

Additionally, this project set a new standard for developing internal sites on SharePoint in Europe and the USA, surpassing the limitations imposed by WordPress technology and policies on internal sites.
