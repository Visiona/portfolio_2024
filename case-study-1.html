Accenture Promotions

Scroll

My Role: User Experience, Front-end Development
Team size: 15 (Project manager, project owner)



CONTEXT

Employees eagerly anticipate promotion announcements to congratulate those who succeeded. Accenture planned to make this a quarterly event, increasing the number of names by 20 times. The existing site was not user-friendly for searching among such large numbers. Our client sought to enhance this experience with technology improvements for better performance and usability.



GOALS

  • Include all employees globally: Expand the list to include up to 100,000 employees worldwide every quarter (before about 5,000 at irregular intervals).
  • Expand filtering options: so the users can find anyone they work with, colleagues at different levels easily without missing a chance to congratulate a colleague.
  • Create easy user experience: Ensure that the large and complex dataset is quick to load and easy to navigate.
  • One site to target two audiences - Leadership levels and all other levels.


RESEARCH

Before I began the process of redesigning the Promotions site, I first needed to understand how employees engage with it and understand what they need in order to find their colleagues in large lists of names.


Survey

I have surveyed 16 people from various departments. Here is a sample of main questions I asked:

  • What department are you in?
  • Who did you look for last time in the promotions list and why?
  • Was it easy to find the person? If not, why?
  • What filtering feature would help you in finding the person among 100,000 names?
  • What challenges do you recall when using filtering tools within corporate sites and outside?

Card sorting

I arranged a meeting with HR to recognize what type of data they are able to provide. It was clear we cannot use all of them as ie. dozens of filters would be completely complicating and obstructing the purpose of the site. I listed 15 types of available data and asked employees to organize them according to the usefulness for their search.

JOURNEY MAP

Key Findings

  • They need various ways to narrow down the search as the available results are overwhelming
  • Self-explanatory interface is needed, so any implementation of complexities can be understood in seconds

WIREFRAMES & USABILITY TESTING

Based on all research so far, I created wireframes to match all feedback from users, and standard usability heuristics. I then invited a new cohort of 6 employees, again at varying levels and locations asked them to navigate these wireframes during sharing screen session on Microsoft Teams.

User's feedback

The users found it easy to accomplish what the interviewer was asking while they also raised the following issues and suggestions:

  • It was not clear that user’s city is missing because there are no promotions at that location
  • Two users struggle to find their team - as it is impossible to list all the teams, this may indicate need for 1-2 more additional filters
  • Some users have international teams and pointed the lack of multi-selection of cities or countries
  • Two users wanted to know which names are part of Accenture, Avanade, AFS - once selected in filters, they are undistinguished within results

Stakeholders' feedback

  • HR asked to hide number of results
  • HR requested to restrict possibility of obtaining downloadable list with all employees in any format

How we solved the problem

  • I used technology that Accenture already has access which doesn’t accrue additional long term costs. These are: Sharepoint as aspx file, HTML, CSS, JavaScript. External third party solutions costs reach over 50,000€ per annum
  • Implemented 10 interactive tailored interdependent multi-select filters, mimicking patterns familiar from Ms Excel which expands ways to search fo colleagues
  • Adding Searching by name
  • Embraced privacy - although the list is hosted on accessible Sharepoint,it is entirely hidden from any access
  • Created simplified 7-step maintenance process for non-coders to update the list
  • Added feedback form to enhance experience in following three quarterly cycles
  • Names linked with person’s profile on internal sites

HIGH FIDELITY MOCKUP INTERACTION

Here are some features incorporated into the prototype based on the wireframe-testing results.

Selecting one option can deactivate filters which options are not present in search results. Applied multi selection system.

Selecting one option removes options in other filters that are not present among search results.

After selection in filters popup is done, short reminder as to what was chosen can be referred on the results site as filter chips. There are two different results view screens, for Leadership & Levels 5 and below.

Exposed short message to address the fact that user’s city is missing as there are no promotions there (previously hidden in tooltip which users missed most time)

Service/Group Level 1 icons applied for simple distinction of names assigned to Avanade, Accenture or AFS projects.

As the site is sitting in Sharepoint, we addressed HR restriction by creating library view for SP Owners while everyone else are unable to access file with list of employees. All files are stored locally.

RESULTS

How the project affected users and the business

Occasional feedback surveys consistently showed high ratings. Visitor numbers grew over 100%, reaching over 160k views in the first week of December FY24. This success attracted attention from other clients, who began using this site as a benchmark for their projects and as inspiration for building their own lists and tools.

Additionally, this project set a new standard for developing internal sites on SharePoint in Europe and the USA, surpassing the limitations imposed by WordPress technology and policies on internal sites.
